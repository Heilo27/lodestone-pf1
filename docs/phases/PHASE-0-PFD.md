# Lodestone — Phase 0: Product & Feature Definition (PFD)

**Date:** 2026-03-31  
**Status:** Draft  
**Working Names:** Lodestone PF1, Lodestone PF2, Lodestone SF1

---

## Problem Statement

Users struggle to maintain consistent, sustainable fitness routines due to:
1. **Motivation decay** — Initial enthusiasm fades without visible progress or accountability
2. **Decision fatigue** — Lack of structured guidance on what to do, when, and how
3. **Isolation** — Solo fitness work lacks social connection and peer accountability
4. **Progress opacity** — Difficulty seeing real impact from training (especially strength/endurance)
5. **Sustainability concerns** — Apps built for quick wins, not long-term habit formation

---

## Solution Overview

**Lodestone** — A three-app ecosystem designed to anchor long-term fitness habits through **progressive structure**, **visible progress**, and **community connection**.

### App Breakdown

| App | Audience | Core Function | Platform |
|-----|----------|---------------|----------|
| **Lodestone PF1** | Solo practitioners | Personal training logs, progress visualization, habit anchoring | iOS/macOS |
| **Lodestone PF2** | Small teams (2-4) | Shared workouts, peer accountability, progress comparison | iOS/macOS |
| **Lodestone SF1** | Coaches, studios | Class management, athlete tracking, programming | iOS/macOS (admin focus) |

---

## Key Principles

1. **Progress is visible** — Every session feeds a meaningful visualization
2. **Habits anchor habits** — Leverage existing routines (morning coffee, lunch break) as fitness triggers
3. **Social without friction** — Easy to share progress, invite accountability partners, no performance pressure
4. **Coach-informed design** — Built with input from strength coaches, not just app designers
5. **Data stays yours** — Privacy-first, on-device first, cloud-optional
